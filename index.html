ABOUT US

Center for Positive Futures (CPF) is an educational institution that envisions a community of compassionate, loving, and respectful citizens and children who can surpass the qualifications of an advancing world. CPF imbibes in its students and faculty members the values of putting God in all endeavors, upholding the truth, respecting others’ beliefs and differences, taking care of the environment, all while carrying out the values of a Filipino citizen. This culture is embodied in all its Six branches: Palawan, Nueva Ecija, San Mateo (Banaba and Guitnang Bayan) and Montalban, Rizal. CPF faculty members are well-versed in understanding and valuing commitment to the students, to learning, and to the school itself. They are called for to help the students in achieving a holistic personal and spiritual development by imbibing in them the importance of living with Christian values, the knowledge of the news of salvation, the essence of charity, compassion, and justice, and most of all, the value of self-worth. CPF makes sure that the students are encouraged to reach their maximum academic and spiritual potential by imparting the knowledge the children needed for their holistic development all while developing a strong entrepreneurial spirit. The institution provides opportunities for the students to be able to practice responsibility at home, in school and in the community. With this, they are trained to become well-rounded and competitive members of the society and aims to take part in achieving a better world.

HISTORY OF CPF

CPF was established on January 2001 in Loyola Heights, Quezon City and started when a group of parents desired a holistic and relevant education for their children. It was first built as a research extension to St. Joseph’s College of Quezon City and was partnered with the college for two years. SJC served as an institutional character to the school. In 2003, the SJC located in Esteban Abada was closed which ended the role of CPF as its research extension. The parents decided to continue its operations as a separate institution, this time, to serve the children living in the periphery of Metro Manila providing an access to private, Catholic and non-traditional education. The very first schools established by CPF were located in San Mateo and Montalban, Rizal. During this time, CPF served children of professionals, employees, industrial and service workers and entrepreneurs. With this, the institution started to instill in its students the entrepreneurial culture which encouraged the children to be responsible and resourceful. In 2012, the school has found out a new way to introduce the lessons. It has used interactive learning through the use of the Smart TV. The teachers have created and researched on media materials to teach the lessons. This has motivated the children to learn in school. In 2013, another method of teaching was introduced and it is with the use of personal tablets and smartphones. Instructional materials from Department of Education and other materials researched by the CPF faculty were developed. These were supplemented with web links to other sites related to the topics. These materials were then transferred to the students’ tablets and phones for easier access and use. Within this year, the students are not just the ones learning but also the parents. CPF also conducted an enhancement program for the basic computer skills to enable the parents to monitor their children’s progress in school. Today, CPF does not stop with the materials and methods they have. The institution is still striving for other ways to incorporate knowledge in technology and providing ways to promote a holistic development not just for its students but also for the parents and faculty members.

ACTIVITIES

Discover the different clubs, and Activities that we offer.


Multi-media Learning/Teaching

CPF trains its students to be competent and knowledgeable with the advancing technological innovations by providing digital learning materials. The teachers utilize the digital tools such as computer units, tablets, and even smartphones to impart knowledge and skills to their students. They create their presentations in Microsoft Powerpoint and transferred to the devices used by the students. This is in line with CPF’s vision of a paperless community. Multi-media teaching/learning encourages the children to learn basic technological knowledge and skills.


Student Trainings and Development

Guidance Counsellor Limit
CPF makes sure that the students are not just physically and mentally healthy but also emotionally and psychologically. The institution provides psychological testings, Standard Aptitude Test (OLSAT), Personality Test (PEC) and counseling conducted by the centralized Guidance Counselor. This is to ensure that the students are equipped with their counseling needs.
Camping/Field Trips
CPF believes that learning is not just confined in the classroom. Learning by experience is also essential for the children’s development. The institution conducts local and national field trips and also field classes to impart experiential knowledge to children.


Parent/Community Program

CPF encourages parents to strive for their children’s academic development. The institution believes that insufficient resources cannot be considered as hindrance for education. With this, CPF came up with the idea of utilizing the resources the parents have to be able to let their children continue with their schooling. The institution innovates methods to generate financial resources from the livelihood and nearby community sources.
PTP (Pawis, Tiyaga, Pag-aalay)
Parents who use agriculture as their livelihood are allowed to pay their children’s tuition fees by sharing their produce to CPF. These products are then sold to the nearby markets to generate income. Some parents who do not have these kind of works are the ones asked to sell the products in the market. This encourages cooperation and hard work among all parents.

Spiritual Formation

One of CPF’s core values is Godliness. The institution’s most essential role to the students is providing spiritual guidance and development. It has been established in the CPF community that everyone treats others with respect and consideration. Forgiveness and peace are also taught in the institution. These values are promoted by CPF through annual recollection and retreat. The institution also established the teachers’ hour and/or students’ hour which enables the students and faculty members to pray and give thanks for the blessings they are receiving every day. The students are also encouraged to share about their experiences that led to their spiritual development and inspire their classmates to achieve this. The whole school are also required to attend the monthly Holy Mass conducted by the partner priests of the school for thanksgiving.

Admissions

Requirements for Students and Program Overview.

requirements

REQUIREMENTS
Applicants must fulfill the following requirements:
1.) Must have a grade not lower than 75% in all subjects.
2.) Must have passed the entrance examination
3.) Form 138 (Report Card) duly signed by the principal
4.) Certificate of good moral character duly signed by the principal/guidance counselor/adviser
5.) Photo copies of Baptismal Certificate and NSO Birth Certificate
6.) Three (3) 1x1 colored ID pictures
7.) Foreign students must submit certification of eligibility from Department of Education. Credits earned abroad must be assessed and certified by the Department of Education.
8.) Students who have withdrawn or have been subjected to disciplinary action are not eligible for readmission in any CPF campus.

Procedures

Old Students:
1.) Submit original Report Card and 4 ID pictures size 1”x1”.
2.) Fill up the enrollment form which is available at the Registrar’s office.
3.) Pay the enrollment fee to the Cashier.

New students / transferees:
1.) Submit complete requirements to the Administration for evaluation
2.) Pay testing fee at the Cashier’s Office
3.) Present official receipt to the Administration Office for entrance examination.
4.) Successful applicants will be given APPROVAL OF APPLICATION FOR ADMISSION by the Registrar.
5.) Submit Approval of Application for Admission.
6.) Fill up enrollment form which is available at the Registrar’s Office
7.) Pay enrollment fee at the Cashier’s office.
